Black Beef Stew Recipe

Black Beef Stew is a classic Malaysian dish featuring tender beef cooked in a rich, dark, and flavorful gravy. Infused with aromatic spices and sweet soy sauce, this dish offers a perfect balance of sweet, savory, and spiced flavors. The combination of fragrant pandan leaves and a medley of traditional herbs makes it a comforting and irresistible choice for special occasions or everyday meals.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef (slice against the grain to ensure tenderness)
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised
  • 1 pandan leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 5 cardamom pods
  • 2 star anise
  • 2 cloves
  • 2 tablespoons chili paste
  • 1 tomato, thinly sliced
  • Sweet soy sauce (to taste)

For Marination:

  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sweet soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground fennel

For the Paste (Finely Ground):

  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 4 shallots
  • 1 inch ginger

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Beef: Slice the beef against the grain to ensure tenderness after cooking.
  2. Marinate the Beef: Mix the beef with the finely ground paste and the marinade ingredients. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (the longer, the better).
  3. Sauté the Spices: Heat some oil in a wok. Add the lemongrass, pandan leaf, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, star anise, and cloves. Sauté until fragrant.
  4. Cook the Chili Paste: Add the chili paste and sauté until the oil separates from the mixture.
  5. Add Tomato: Add the sliced tomato into the wok and cook until soft.
  6. Cook the Beef: Add the marinated beef to the wok. Stir well and add sweet soy sauce to taste.
  7. Tenderize the Beef: Cook the beef until tender and the sauce thickens.
Tip:

The longer you marinate the beef, the more flavorful it becomes as the marinade penetrates the meat, making it soft and tender.
