Spicy Sambal Prawns Recipe

Spicy Sambal Prawns is a savory and flavorful Malaysian dish, combining succulent prawns with a rich and spicy sambal sauce. Perfect for seafood lovers, this dish is a crowd favorite for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • ½ kg prawns
  • A pinch of salt
  • ½ cup cooking oil
  • 3 large onions
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 4-5 small shallots
  • ½ inch ginger
  • 10-15 dried chilies
  • A little water
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 3 tbsp chili sauce
  • Tamarind juice (diluted)
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar or palm sugar
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Sambal Paste:
    • Thinly slice 1 large onion for sautéing.
    • Cut another large onion into thick circular slices for garnish later.
    • Roughly chop the remaining onion, garlic, small shallots, and ginger.
    • Snip the dried chilies into small pieces and boil them until soft.
    • Blend the boiled dried chilies, chopped ingredients, and a bit of water into a smooth paste.
    • Prepare tamarind juice by mixing tamarind with hot water and squeezing to create a thick juice.
  2. Preparing the Prawns:
    • Trim the prawn heads and clean out the back vein.
    • Wash thoroughly and sprinkle a little salt.
    • Heat oil in a wok and fry the prawns until half-cooked.
    • Flip quickly once the side turns red. Remove and set aside.
  3. Cooking the Sambal:
    • Reuse the oil from frying the prawns for cooking the sambal.
    • Sauté the sliced onion until soft.
    • Add the blended sambal paste and cook until the oil separates and the sambal turns darker in color.
    • Add water to the blender, swirl, and pour into the wok. Cook until the sambal thickens and the oil separates again.
    • Mix in the oyster sauce, chili sauce, tamarind juice, and brown sugar. Adjust sweetness to taste.
    • Once the sambal is ready, add the prawns and stir gently until cooked.
    • Lastly, sprinkle the circular onion slices and cook until the onions soften.
Tip:

Use large onions for a tastier sambal. Be cautious with dried chilies as their heat levels vary. Adjust to your spice tolerance!
